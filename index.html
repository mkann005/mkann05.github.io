My name is Manisha Kannan

3rd year biology major at UC Riverside

I grew up in Irvine, California, where my interest in medicine and healthcare first took root. Now, as a premed student, I’m working hard to turn that interest into a lifelong career. One of the most rewarding parts of my journey so far has been volunteering as a skills instructor in an Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) class. As a nationally certified EMT, I have the privilege of guiding and training students who are eager to enter the field. Teaching has allowed me to reinforce my own knowledge and share the skills and experiences that make a difference in emergency care. I love being able to support students as they learn essential, hands-on skills that will enable them to handle real-life medical situations with confidence and care. In addition to my role as an instructor, I’ve volunteered at Riverside Community Hospital, assisting nurses and helping provide patient care. This experience has given me a deeper understanding of the demands and rewards of healthcare work. Supporting nurses and interacting with patients taught me the importance of empathy and teamwork in a medical setting. Witnessing the dedication of healthcare providers and seeing firsthand the impact they have on their patients has only strengthened my determination to pursue a career in medicine. Looking ahead, I’m excited about the possibility of attending medical school and specializing in dermatology. I’m drawn to dermatology because it combines scientific knowledge with the chance to make a tangible impact on people’s lives, helping patients feel comfortable and confident in their own skin. I know the journey ahead will be challenging, but each experience has confirmed that this is the path I’m meant to take. I’m ready to keep learning and working toward my goal of becoming a dermatologist, providing care and support for patients in a field I am passionate about
